DUCATI 1000-RL

AUTOMATIC POWER FACTOR CORRECTION SYSTEMS

INSTRUCTION MANUAL

DANGER !

BEFORE MAKING THE FIRST START-UP, CHECK THE TIGHTENING OF ALL TERMINALS

BEFORE ACCESS INSIDE THE EQUIPMENT, PLEASE CARRY OUT THE FOLLOWING :

  1. SHUT OFF POWER.
  2. WAIT AT LEAST 5 MINUTE.
  3. DISCHARGE THE CAPACITORS BY GROUNDING THE TERMINALS TO SHORT-CIRCUIT THEM.

Safety

"DUCATI 1000-RL" automatic power factor correction equipments are manufactured and tested in accordance with current standards outgoing from our factory in perfect conditions of technical safety. In order to maintain these conditions and ensure safe operations, the user must strictly adhere the instructions herein.

Before start-up it is necessary to verify that the characteristics of the network are compatible with the equipment rating data and. Be sure to carry out a good ground/PE connections.

WARNING!

This equipment must be installed by qualified personnel, following the applicable regulations currently , in order to prevent injury or damage to persons or property. Maintenance and repair work must be entrusted solely to authorized personnel. Before undergoing any maintenance or repairs, the equipment must be disconnected from all power sources.

Definitions

CAPACITOR BANK: an assembly made up of capacitors and their respective switching and protection devices: the bank as a whole is controlled by one of the output relays of the automatic controller.

STEPS: the number of units into which the total power of the equipment is divided, depending on how it is managed by the controller; a single step may be made up of one or more banks.

Description

The automatic power factor correction systems belonging to the DUCATI 1000-RL series have been specifically designed for centralized power factor correction of industrial loads. They are equipped with a controller (N1) sensitive to reactive power, which in the automatic operating mode governs the power factor correction equipment, switching capacitor bank STEPS in and out in order to maintain the power factor at the optimal level set. In the manual operating mode, each capacitor bank can be switched in irrespective of the network’s demand for reactive power.

To work properly the controller must receive a voltage signal and current signal from the network

Construction features

Capacitor Bank

Inside the equipment there are two to ten capacitor drawers of the DUCATI C100-L type with a maximum power of 100kVAr.
Each drawer is composed of single-phase 'MONO' series capacitors, battery insertion contactors, 189Hz tuned filter reactors and protection fuses. The capacitors are provided with fixed discharge resistors (discharge at 1/10 of the voltage

Detuned Reactor

  • tuning frequency: 189 or 141 Hz

  • linearity up to 2 Ip / In

  • harmonic distortion in permissible current: 30% In at 250 Hz; 15% In at 350 Hz

  • merit factor: Q> 20

  • materials in class H

  • overtemperature of operation: 90 ° C

  • thermal protection through thermistor with NC contact.

Contactor and fuses

The operation of the capacitor banks is carried out by means of contactors suitable for insertion of capacitive loads and equipped with pre-insertion resistors. Protection against short circuits is ensured by a triad of NH-00 fuses type gl for each capacitor bank

Main Circuit Breaker / Disconnector switch

The equipment is equipped with a three-pole disconnector switch with door locking device for each 800mm wide column constituting the equipment. In addition, the disconnector is equipped with a protective cover for the input terminals. The input of the power cables is provided from above on each disconnector.

Automatic power factor controller

The Controller, model R8, in automatic mode takes control of the power factor correction equipment, providing for the insertion and disconnection of the STEP of capacitor banks. In manual operation mode, it is possible to insert capacitor banks manually independently of the reactive power demand of the network. It also allows you to set the value of the desired power factor correction factor.

For correct operation, it must receive a voltage and a current signal from the grid.

The regulator insertion is of the VARMETRICAL type and receives power inside the equipment.

The current signal coming from the network TA ... / 5A must be brought to the terminals (K, L).

Installation

Positioning

The equipment is designed for indoor installation, on floors with cable entry from above or below on each disconnector.

It is not allowed to enter in the cabinet with the feeding cables, drilling other parts of the panel if not the upper or lower entry plates.

Given the installed power, it is advisable to keep the panel walls at a safe distance from the wall or other equipment in order to allow adequate ventilation.

On the outside of the equipment there is a small tower for each column, EV, for the extraction of hot air: do not obstruct the air inlet holes.

Mains connection

Connect the L1, L2, L3 poles of the input Disconnector switch (QS1) to the power supply, using copper conductors with a suitable cross-section. The power supply line of the equipment must always be protected against overload and short circuit by using fuses or a thermal magnetic circuit breaker. If the plant conditions require it, the protection against earth fault must also be installed. The use of this protection is recommended even when not required by current regulations. Also connect the cabinet ground to the PE ground conductor.

Connecting the current input signal

Connect to the terminals (K, L) the secondary of the CT ... / 5A., Using a single-core cable with a section not lower than 2.5mm2. Normally a CT with 5VA performance, class 1 is sufficient. If the CT is installed far from the equipment or other instruments are inserted in the circuit, the voltage drop of the cable and the amperometric consumption of the installed instruments must be evaluated.

WARNING!

The reactive power controller must be connected as a varmeter, placing the current transformer on L1 phase. The voltage signal is drawn internally from the panelboard between phases L2 and L3 (middle and right poles of the main switch) :
!! RESPECT the L1,L2,L3 sequence connecting the main cables !!
The mains CT must therefore placed on the network on the phase L1 (left pole) .
Connection errors will prevent the reactive power controller from correctly interpreting the measurement signals, thus undermining the effectiveness of the power factor correction system.
In addition:

  • The C.T. must be connected both upstream from the power factor correction system and upstream from the load (See picture on next page, ref “a” and “b”).
  • The C.T. must never be connected directly to the power line supplying the load (See picture ref. “c”) or directly on the power line of the power factor correction system (See picture ref. “d”).
  • Any fixed power factor correction systems installed in the plant must be powered upstream from the measuring C.T.

Setup and controls

Before putting the appliance into service, the following settings and checks must be carried out:

Setup

See the instruction manual of the R8 automatic controller.

Controls

It must be ascertained that:

  • There are no loose connections, checking all the tightenings, or interrupted fuses (to be repeated one month after commissioning)

  • The CT placed on the network is inserted upstream of the connection point of the equipment and the loads to be corrected.

  • The CT is mounted on the phase connected to the L1 pole (left pole) of the main switch and there are no short circuit interruptions or bridges in the relevant secondary circuit.

  • Possible fixed capacitors are not connected downstream of the measurement CT.

  • To facilitate the interpretation of the indications provided by the regulator during the subsequent operations of putting the equipment into service, it would be useful to be able to temporarily dispose of the following load states:

    • absence of any load;

    • load with inductive displacement capable of providing a higher reactive power (minimum 1.5 times) than that supplied by the first capacitor bank;

    • capacitors existing downstream of the measuring CT disconnected.

Start-Up/Commissioning

The commissioning of the R8 regulator is very important, and it is advisable to refer to the manual for all relevant information.

We remind here that the regulator performs a measurement of the power of the individual condenser steps installed in the equipment, and chooses the sequence and characteristics of the use of these steps.

In the event of a malfunction, make sure that the installation is correct and that the loads are current and consult the controller manual. Currents below 100mA on the secondary circuit of the network CT block the operation of the regulator and disable all functions.

Maintenance

Contactors

The wear on the main contacts and condition of the switching resistors must be checked at reasonable intervals, and the resistors (or the entire contactor) replaced accordingly, the number of switching operations will strongly depend on the fluctuations in the load subject to power factor correction.

Contactor inspections every 10,000 switching operations ( see R8 User’s Manual )

WARNING!

It is very important to perform the checks described above in order to preclude the occurrence, due to excessive wear of the contacts, of dangerous operating conditions which may result in the destruction of contactors and capacitors.
TO PREVENT SOME DAMAGES, DO NOT OPEN THE MAIN SWITCH IF THERE ARE SOME WORKING BANKS ON THE EQUIPMENT:
SET THE REGULATOR IN MANUAL MODE AND DISCONNECT ALL THE BANKS BEFORE SWITCH-OFF THE MAIN DISCONNECTOR
ON FOLLOWING START-UP, SET THE REGULATOR ON AUTOMATIC MODE

The number of contactor operations likely to take place while the power factor correction system is working may be estimated at the time of installation by observing how many switching operations are performed by the contactors of the different capacitor banks to follow the load over a given period of time. For example, if it is found that a bank carries out 6 switching-in operations per hour in response to load fluctuations and considering a work shift of 8 hours per day, the contacts will have to be checked for wear after approximately a year.

6 operations per hour x 8 hours x 220 days @ 10,000 operations.
