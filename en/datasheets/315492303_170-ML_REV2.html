DUCATI 170-ML

**AUTOMATIC POWER FACTOR CORRECTION SYSTEMS **

Instruction Manual - (rev. 2)

Versions
25,5 ÷ 170 kVAr 400V (rete 400Vac) 415.04.21xxN

DANGER!

When connected to the mains supply, this unit contains dangerous voltages, which may be fatal.

Ensure all power is off and wait at least 2 minutes.

BEFORE ACCESS INSIDE THE EQUIPMENT,FOLLOW SAFETY OPERATIONS

General

The “DUCATI 170-ML” automatic power factor correction systems have been built and tested in accordance with the standards in force and have come from production works in perfect condition as regards technical safety.

So as to maintain these conditions and guarantee safe running, the user must follow the indications in the present instructions for use.

Please ensure your electrical contractor installs this unit in accordance with European and/or International Standards and local Supply Authority “Service and Installation” Rules.

Warning

This equipment must be installed by qualified staff only following the systems standards and regulations, with the purpose of avoiding damages to anyone.

Maintenance or repair operations should be managed only by authorized personnel

Definitions

  • CAPACITOR BANK: an assembly made up of capacitors and their respective switching and protection devices: the bank as a whole is controlled by one of the output relays of the automatic controller.

  • STEPS: the number of units into which the total power of the equipment is divided, depending on how it is managed by the controller; a single step may be made up of one or more banks.

Description

The automatic power factor correction systems of the “DUCATI 170-ML” series has been designed for local power factor correction in typical industry application.

These are fitted with a PF digital controller (N1) which regulate the desired power factor in AUTOMATIC mode switching the available banks by the output relay.

In MANUAL mode, each output can be switched ON or OFF without look on the network reactive power.

The reactive power measurement is made a “varmetric” method, by the way is necessary to supply a current signal using a CURRENT TRANSFORMER installed on the network by the final user.

Components

The equipment includes the following components:

  • Switch disconnector.
  • Capacitors banks in delta connection, fuse protected.
  • Detuning reactors at 189Hz (7%).
  • Single phase capacitor elements with discharge resistors.
  • Five outputs microprocessor PF controller R5 model,
  • Contactors designed for switching reactive loads, 230Vac coil supply

Installation

Physical connection

Place the cubicle as close as possible to the main switchboard, taking into account the requirements of EN 61439 standards series. Please consider also the power losses generated by a detuned power factor correction system (approx 6/7 watts/kVAr) , require a well-ventilated room. Please check clearances opening doors and checking space with other equipment.

Mains connection

The rating of this cable should be matched to the protection on the main switchboard e.g. If a 400 Amp circuit breaker is mounted on the main switchboard cable with minimum 400 Amp rating should be used regardless of the Power factor Correction Size.

Connect terminals L1, L2, L3 of the switch disconnector (-Q1) using appropriate cable size considering a 20% oversize due the harmonic effects, in any case take a look at ANNEX H in EN 61439-1. Assure a reliable PE connection to the earth network.

Current transformer

The current transformer should monitor the WHOLE load current (or the load to be corrected) PLUS the power factor equipment current and should be physically placed above (toward the supply) the connections for the PFC system.

The CT should be placed in L1 phase, if this is not possible, control connections should be changed in the PFC system. E.g. If the CT is in the L2 phase then swap L1 and L2 phase on the PFC side of the switch disconnector. The CT shall have 5 amps range on the secondary side. Note the CT cable must connect in series with the existing CT cable.

Commissioning

It is important for correct operation and long life of the equipment that the following procedures are carried out, failure to set up the controller correctly could result in improper operation and premature failure of components.

Signal currents lower than 100mA on the secondary circuit of the CT, cut off all the R5 operation and disabling all automatic functions

The commissioning is easy to perform, just the CT ratio parameter must to set on the power factor regulator, all the follows automatic operations will be made by the software.

Please refer to the R5 USER MANUAL for detailed informations.

Maintenance

It is essential that Power Factor Systems are checked at regular intervals to ensure long life of equipment and that optimum savings are achieved on your electricity account.

The R5 controller allows to perform some checks without opening the cabinet.

Each 12 months is recommended to perform control activities that should include, but not be limited to, the following :

Enclosure Exterior

  • Ensure enclosure is receiving adequate ventilation. Check to see if filters are clean and airflow is not restricted. Replace filters and remove obstacles as required.
  • Ensure enclosure, enclosure doors, louvers, and rodent guards are adequate to prevent entry of liquids, insects, and rodents. Clean and correct as required.
  • Examine warning signs and placards. If not legible, replace.

Enclosure Interior

  • Remove accumulated dust and dirt.
  • heck proper operation of ventilation equipment (fans) and thermostats.
  • Clean all enclosure windows.

Bus Bar & Wiring

  • Inspect for loose bus bar connections and discoloration. Tighten as required.
  • Inspect control wire connections, tighten as required.
  • Inspect wire insulation for cuts, breakdown, or burns. Replace as required.

Capacitors

  • Verify overpressure devices visually checking if the cans is extended or not
  • Verify capacitance of the banks by measurement device or checking the rating current during a manual mode operation, verify with specification.
  • Verify discharge resistors are working properly. Replace as required.

Fuses

  • Check all capacitor fuses, control fuses, and PT fuses for blown fuses. Replace as required.
  • Check all mounting hardware, tighten as required.
  • Confirm proper fuse rating. Verify with specification.

Controls

  • Perform several manual switching operations of each capacitor stage to ensure equipment is in proper working order.
  • Check all indicator lights to ensure proper functioning.
  • Check controller for proper functionality to specification.

Air Disconnect Switch

  • Check torque on all bolts. Tighten as required.
  • Check for proper operation
  • Check that all contact are free of pitting and corrosion.

Harmonic Filter Reactors

  • Confirm nameplate rating.
  • Check torque on all bolts. Tighten as required.
  • Check for physical damage.
