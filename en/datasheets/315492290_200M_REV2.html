Safety

“DUCATI 18-M” and “DUCATI 200-M” automatic power factor correction systems have been manufactured and tested in accordance with current regulations and left the factory in perfect conditions of technical safety.

In order to maintain these conditions and ensure safe operation, the user must strictly abide by the instructions herein.

Before start-up it is necessary to verify that the characteristics of the network supplying power to the equipment are compatible with the equipment rating data and that the equipment is properly grounded via an adequately sized cable

Warning

This equipment must be installed by qualified personnel, in observance of the applicable regulations currently in force, in order to prevent injury or damage to persons or property. Maintenance and repair work must be entrusted solely to authorized personnel.

Before undergoing any maintenance or repairs, the equipment must be disconnected from all power sources.

Definitions

  • CAPACITOR BANK: an assembly made up of capacitors and their respective switching and protection devices: the bank as a whole is controlled by one of the output relays of the automatic controller.

  • STEPS: the number of units into which the total power of the equipment is divided, depending on how it is managed by the controller; a single step may be made up of one or more banks.

Description

The automatic power factor correction systems belonging to the DUCATI 18-M and DUCATI 200-M series have been specifically designed for centralized power factor correction of industrial loads.

They are equipped with a controller (N1) sensitive to reactive power, which in the automatic operating mode governs the power factor correction equipment, switching capacitor bank STEPS in and out in order to maintain the power factor at the optimal level set.

In the manual operating mode, each capacitor bank can be switched in irrespective of the network’s demand for reactive power.

To work properly the controller must receive a voltage signal and current signal from the network.

Components

The equipment includes the following components:

  • Omnipolar underload switch, four-pole for powers up to 80 kVAr, three-pole for higher powers.
  • Triads of NH-00 type fuses to protect the capacitor banks.
  • Capacitor banks consisting of delta-connected single-phase elements. Each bank is equipped with specific fixed discharge resistors.
  • Automatic microprocessor controller capable of managing up to 5 capacitor banks.
  • Specific three-pole switching contactors for switching in capacitive loads (coil at 230Vac).

Installation

Positioning

The equipment is designed to be installed indoors on a wall, with top cable entry.

It must be installed in a well-ventilated place and air must be able to circulate freely through the vents.

Instructions for wall mounting and cabinet removal

  1. Fasten screw anchors (not supplied) to the wall as shown in the diagram provided

  2. Mount the unit

  3. Remove the upper and lower fastenings

  4. Take off the cabinet

Mains connection

Connect poles L1, L2, L3 and N (if present) of the incoming switch (-Q1) to the mains power network using suitably sized copper wires.

The equipment power supply line must always be protected against overloads and short circuits by means of fuses or a circuit breaker sized for 1.43xIn of the power factor correction panel.

Wherever plant conditions thus require, ground fault protection must also be installed. Such protection is also recommended even when not required by current regulations.

Also connect the ground terminal of the panel board to the ground wire.

Connecting the current input signal

Connect the secondary winding of the CT .../5A to the terminals (K, L) using a single-pole cable with a cross-section of at least 2.5mm2.

Normally a CT with a 5VA, class 1 performance rating is sufficient. If the CT is installed far from the controller or other instruments are connected to the circuit, it will be necessary to assess the voltage drop of the cable and the current consumption of the instruments installed.

The reactive power controller must be connected as a varmeter, picking up the current signal on one phase and the voltage signal on the other two phases. The voltage signal is drawn internally from the panelboard between phases L2 and L3 (middle and right poles of the main switch)

Warning

The mains CT must therefore be applied on the phase connected to pole L1 (left pole) of the main incoming switch. Connection errors will prevent the reactive power controller from correctly interpreting the measurement signals, thus undermining the effectiveness of the power factor correction system. In addition:

  • The C.T. must be connected both upstream from the power factor correction system and upstream from the load (See figure, positions a or b).
  • The C.T. must never be connected directly to the power line supplying the load (See figure, position c) or directly on the power line of the power factor correction system (See figure, position d).
  • Any fixed power factor correction systems installed in the plant must be powered upstream from the measuring C.T.

Commissioning

For instructions on putting the equipment into service, see the “R5” controller manual provided

If the equipment fails to work, check whether it has been correctly installed and check the status of the loads: currents below 100mA on the secondary circuit of the mains CT will prevent the controller from working and disable all of its functions.

So at this stage the loads must be running.

Maintenance

Contactors

The wear on the main contacts and condition of the switching resistors must be checked at reasonable intervals, and the resistors (or the entire contactor) replaced accordingly, bearing in mind that the number of switching operations will strongly depend on the fluctuations in the load subject to power factor correction.

  • Contactor inspections every 10,000 switching operations

Warning

It is very important to perform the checks described above in order to preclude the occurrence, due to excessive wear of the contacts, of dangerous operating conditions such as the destruction of contactors and capacitors.

The number of contactor operations likely to take place while the power factor correction system is working may be estimated at the time of installation by observing how many switching operations are performed by the contactors of the different capacitor banks to follow the load over a given period of time. For example, if it is found that a bank carries out 6 switching-in operations per hour in response to load fluctuations and considering a work shift of 8 hours per day, the contacts will have to be checked for wear after approximately a year.

6 operations per hour x 8 hours x 220 days @ 10,000 operations.
