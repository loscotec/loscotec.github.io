DUCATI F60

A - Safety

The F60 three-phase modular unit was manufactured and tested in conformity with current standards and left the factory in perfect conditions of technical safety.

In order to maintain these conditions and ensure safe operation, the user must strictly abide by the instructions herein.

WARNING !

This equipment must be installed by qualified personnel, in observance of the applicable regulations currently in force, in order to prevent injury or damage to persons or property.

Maintenance and repair work must be entrusted solely to authorized personnel.

Before undergoing any maintenance or repairs, the equipment must be disconnected from all power sources.

DUCATI ENERGIA s.p.a. disclaims liability for any injury or damage caused to persons or property as a result of improper use of its products.

In view of the continuous evolution of our technology, we reserve the right to change the specifications contained herein without notice. Thus the catalogue descriptions and data cannot be construed as contractually binding.

C - Installing an unit or a bank of factory-assembled units

  1. Remove only the protective cover of the unit on the rubber cable guide side (Fig.3 - Fig.4).
  2. Prepare the power supply cables as directed in section "B".
  3. Perforate the membranes of the cable guides and fit them over the cables.
  4. Fix the unit or bank with diameter 5 screws, using the slotted fastening tabs provided on the base (see template Fig.5).
  5. Fit and tighten the eyelet terminals on the capacitor terminals, making sure to apply the washers as shown in Fig.6-7.
  6. Fit the protective cover in place, making sure that the cable guides are correctly positioned in the seats provided.

WARNING !

Terminal tightening torque 20 N/m (2 kg/m)

D - Assembling a number of units into a bank and electrical connection

(For N capacitors use the N-1 parallel connection kit code 415.69.9910).

  1. Remove the covers and rubber cable guides of each unit (Note: only 3 cable guides will be used).
  2. Fix the first unit as directed in step C-4
  3. Using a blade, remove the membrane covering the holes on the side opposite the coupling collars (Fig. 9).
  4. Then couple together the various units, removing the membranes to allow the collars to be inserted (Fig. 8). Do not remove the membrane on the last module. Fix the units in place (as described in C-4).
  5. Insert the parallel bars as shown in Fig. 9, making sure to arrange the washers as shown in Fig. 7.
  6. Prepare the power supply cables as directed in "A".
  7. Perforate the membrane of three cable guides and fit them over the cables.
  8. Fit the eyelet terminals of the power supply cables on the terminals of the first unit, making sure to arrange the washers as shown in Fig. 7.
  9. Completely tighten all the terminals of the various units.
  10. Fit the protective covers, starting from the unit opposite the cable entry.
  11. When fitting the last cover, correctly position the rubber cable guides.

E - Construction and technical features

  • Overpressure device.
  • Level of insulation: 3/8 KV.
  • Losses < 0.5 Watt/KVar
  • Protection rating: IP 40.
  • Discharge time 50V after 1 minutes.
  • Indoor installation.
  • Standards EN 60831 – 1/2

CAUTIONARY NOTES

  • Do not exceed a current of 85A for units obtained by assembling a number of modules
  • When using the unit on automatic equipment, take care to check that the regulator intervention times are greater than the capacitor discharge time (50V – 1min.) and that the capacitors are inserted via contactors for class AC6b capacitive loads.
  • Take utmost care to ensure that the cooling air temperature falls within the limits set as per specifications and that the air can circulate freely among the modules.
  • When static AC/DC conversion systems are present in the plant - e.g. for the operation of DC motors, uninterrupted power supplies, etc. - harmonic currents are generated and may cause either current or voltage overloads that the capacitors are unable to withstand In such cases contact the DUCATI ENERGIA technical department, which can supply capacitors and equipment suited to the specific application.
  • The unit is capable of withstanding a normal harmonic load: 1.3 In - 1.1 Vn (sum of the fundamental voltage and harmonic-induced voltage drops).
