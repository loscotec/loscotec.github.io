DUCATI 2400-R

AUTOMATIC POWER FACTOR CORRECTION SYSTEMS**

Instruction Manual - Rev. 1

Versioni

DANGER!

PRIMA DI EFFETTUARE LA PRIMA MESSA IN SERVIZIO VERIFICARE IL SERRAGGIO DI TUTTI TERMINALI

BEFORE SEEKING INTERNAL ACCESS TO THE EQUIPMENT CARRY OUT THE FOLLOWING STEPS:

  1. SHUT OFF POWER.
  2. WAIT AT LEAST 5 MINUTES.
  3. DISCHARGE THE CAPACITORS BY GROUNDING THE TERMINALS TO SHORT-CIRCUIT THEM.

Safety

Equipments series "DUCATI 2400-R" automatic power factor correction have been manufactured and tested in accordance with current standards and left the factory in perfect conditions of technical safety. In order to maintain these conditions and ensure safe operation, the user must strictly abide by the instructions herein.

Before start-up it is necessary to verify that the characteristics of the network supplying power to the equipment are compatible with the equipment rating data and that the equipment is properly grounded via an adequately sized cable.

Warning

This equipment must be installed by qualified personnel, in observance of the applicable regulations currently in force, in order to prevent injury or damage to persons or property. Maintenance and repair work must be entrusted solely to authorized personnel.

Before undergoing any maintenance or repairs, the equipment must be disconnected from all power sources.

Definitions

  • CAPACITOR BANK: an assembly made up of capacitors and their respective switching and protection devices: the bank as a whole is controlled by one of the output relays of the automatic controller.

  • STEPS: the number of units into which the total power of the equipment is divided, depending on how it is managed by the controller; a single step may be made up of one or more banks.

Description

The automatic power factor correction systems belonging to the DUCATI 2400-R series have been specifically designed for centralized power factor correction of industrial loads. They are equipped with a controller (N1) sensitive to reactive power, which in the automatic operating mode governs the power factor correction equipment, switching capacitor bank STEPS in and out in order to maintain the power factor at the optimal level set. In the manual operating mode, each capacitor bank can be switched in irrespective of the network’s demand for reactive power.

To work properly the controller must receive a voltage signal and current signal from the network.

Components

Capacitor banks

Inside the switchboard there are two to ten drawers of DUCATI C240 type capacitors with a maximum power of 160kvar. Each drawer is made up of ‘MONO’ single-phase capacitors, contactors for battery insertion and protection fuses. The capacitors are equipped with fixed discharge resistors (discharge at 1/10 of the nominal voltage in about 30 seconds).

Contactors and fuses

The operation of capacitor banks is carried out by means of contactors suitable for inserting capacitive loads and equipped with pre-insertion resistors. Protection against short-circuits is ensured by a set of three NH-00 gl-type fuses for each capacitor bank

General switch / disconnector

The equipment is equipped with a three-pole under-load switch with door-locking device for each 800mm-wide column constituting the equipment. In addition, the disconnector is equipped with a protective cover for the input terminals. The input of the power cables is foreseen from the top on each disconnector.

Automatic reactive power regulator

The regulator, model R8, in the automatic operating mode takes control of the power factor correction equipment, providing for switching on and off the capacitor battery STEP. In the manual operating mode it allows to insert the capacitor banks manually regardless of the reactive power demand of the network. It also allows you to set the value of the desired power factor correction cosj. For correct operation, it must receive a voltage signal and a current signal from the network. The insertion of the regulator is of the VARMETRIC type and receives power internally to the equipment. The current signal coming from the mains CT .... / 5A must be brought to the terminals (K, L).

Installation

Positioning

The equipment is designed for floor installation, with top or , under request, bottom cable entry.

It must be installed in a well-ventilated place and air must be able to circulate freely. A minimum distance between wall and the rear-side of the equipment shall be min. 30cm is well accepted for maintenance.

Mains connection

Connect poles L1, L2, L3 on single or both incoming switches (-Q1, -Q2) to the power mains using suitably sized copper wires. The same sequence, R, S, T, must be followed for both switches.

The equipment power supply line must always be protected against overloads and short circuits by means of fuses or a circuit breaker.

Wherever plant conditions thus require, ground fault protection must also be installed. Such pro-tection is also recommended even when not required by current regulations.

Also connect the ground terminal of the panelboard to the ground wire.

Connecting the current input signal

Connect the secondary winding of the CT .../1 or /5A to the terminals (K, L) using a single-pole cable with a cross-section of at least 2.5 mm2.

Normally a CT with a 5VA, class 1 performance rating is sufficient. If the CT is installed far from the controller or other instruments are connected to the circuit, it will be necessary to assess the voltage drop of the cable and the current consumption of the instruments installed.

Warning

The reactive power controller must be connected as a varmeter, picking up the current signal on one phase and the voltage signal on the other two phases. The voltage signal is drawn internally from the panelboard between phases L2 and L3 (middle and right poles of the main switch)

The mains CT must therefore be applied on the phase connected to pole L1 (left pole) of the incoming switches. Connection errors will prevent the reactive power controller from correctly interpreting the measurement signals, thus undermining the effectiveness of the power factor correction system. In addition:

  • The C.T. must be connected both upstream from the power factor correction system and upstream from the load (See figure, positions a and b).
  • The C.T. must never be connected directly to the power line supplying the load (See figure, position c) or directly on the power line of the power factor correction system (See figure, ref. “d”).
  • Any fixed power factor correction systems installed in the plant must be powered upstream from the measuring C.T.

Adjustment and Controls

Before commissioning the equipment, make the following adjustments and checks:

Settings

See the instruction manual of the R8 automatic controller.

Controls

Make sure that:

  • There are no loose connections, checking all fastenings, or interrupted fuses (to be repeated one month after commissioning)
  • The CT placed in the network is inserted upstream of the connection point of the equipment and of the loads to be rephased.
  • The CT is mounted on the phase connected to the L1 pole (left pole) of the main switch and there are no interruptions or short-circuit bridges in the relative secondary circuit.
  • Any fixed capacitors are not connected downstream of the measurement CT.
  • To facilitate the interpretation of the indications provided by the regulator during the subsequent equipment commissioning operations, it would be useful to be able to temporarily use the following load states:
    • absence of any load.
    • load with inductive phase shift able to supply a higher reactive power (minimum 1.5 times) than that supplied by the first capacitor bank.
    • existing capacitors downstream of the measurement CT disconnected.

Commissioning

The commissioning of the R8 regulator is very important, and it is advisable to refer to the manual for all relevant information.

We recall here that the regulator performs a measurement of the power of the individual steps of condensers installed in the equipment, and chooses the sequence and characteristics of the use of these steps.

In case of failure to operate, make sure of the correct installation, the state of the loads and consult the regulator manual. Currents lower than 30mA on the secondary circuit of the network CT block the regulator operation and disable all its functions.

Maintenance

Contactors

The wear on the main contacts and condition of the switching resistors must be checked at reasonable intervals, and the resistors (or the entire contactor) replaced accordingly, bearing in mind that the number of switching operations will strongly depend on the fluctuations in the load subject to power factor correction.

  • Contactor inspections every 10,000 switching operations ( see pag.78 in the R8 User’s Manual )

Warning

It is very important to perform the checks described above in order to preclude the occurrence, due to excessive wear of the contacts, of dangerous operating conditions which may result in the destruction of contactors and capacitors.

TO PREVENT SOME DAMAGES, DO NOT OPEN THE MAIN SWITCH IF THERE ARE SOME WORKING BANKS ON THE EQUIPMENT:

SET THE REGULATOR IN MANUAL MODE AND DISCONNECT ALL THE BANKS BEFORE SWITCH-OFF THE MAIN DISCONNECTOR ON FOLLOWING START-UP, SET THE REGULATOR ON AUTOMATIC MODE

The number of contactor operations likely to take place while the power factor correction system is working may be estimated at the time of installation by observing how many switching operations are performed by the contactors of the different capacitor banks to follow the load over a given period of time. For example, if it is found that a bank carries out 6 switching-in operations per hour in response to load fluctuations and considering a work shift of 8 hours per day, the contacts will have to be checked for wear after approximately a year.

6 operations per hour x 8 hours x 220 days @ 10,000 operations.
