CHASSIS CAPACITORS BANKS

User Manual - Rev. 1

DANGER!

When connected to the mains supply, this unit contains dangerous voltages, which may be fatal.

Ensure all power is off and wait at least 5 minutes.

BEFORE ACCESS INSIDE THE EQUIPMENT,FOLLOW SAFETY OPERATIONS

General

The DUCATI racks have been built and tested in accordance with the standards in force and have come from production works in perfect condition as regards technical safety.

So as to maintain these conditions and guarantee safe running, the user must follow the indications in the present instructions for use.

Please ensure your electrical contractor installs this unit in accordance with European and/or International Standards and local Supply Authority “Service and Installation” Rules.

Warning

This equipment must be installed by qualified staff only following the systems standards and regulations, with the purpose of avoiding damages to anyone.

Maintenance or repair operations should be managed only by authorized personnel.

Definitions

  • CAPACITOR BANK*: an assembly made up of capacitors and their respective switching and protection devices: the bank as a whole is controlled by one of the output relays of the automatic controller.

  • STEPS: the number of units into which the total power of the equipment is divided, depending on how it is managed by the controller; a single step may be made up of one or more banks.

Description

Chassis capacitors banks series is the fully tested solution for custom equipment especially for panel-builders. It is a prewired subassembly designed to be placed in power factor correction cubicles, either stand alone or inside a switchboard. It is available with or without integrated bus-bar for different electrical distribution types of network, also fitted with detune reactor for several applications.

Components

The equipment includes the following components:

  • Capacitors banks in delta connection, fuse protected.
  • Detuning reactors at 189Hz (7%) where applicable.
  • Single or three phase capacitor elements with discharge resistors.
  • Contactors designed for switching reactive loads, 230Vac coil supply

Installation

Physical connection

Place the systems inside cabinet/switchboard, taking into account the requirements of EN 61439 standards series. Please consider also the power losses generated by a detuned power factor correction system (approx 6/7 watts/kVAr) , require a well-ventilated room. Please check clearances opening doors and checking space with other equipment.

Mains connection

The rating of this cable should be matched to the protection on the main switchboard e.g. If a 400 Amp circuit breaker is mounted on the main switchboard cable with minimum 400 Amp rating should be used regardless of the Power factor Correction Size.

Connect terminals L1, L2, L3 of the switch disconnector (-Q1) using appropriate cable size considering a 20% oversize due the harmonic effects, in any case take a look at ANNEX H in EN 61439-1. Assure a reliable PE connection to the earth network.

Enclosure

The ambient air temperature surrounding the electrical cubicle must be within the following limits:

  • maximum temperature: 40 °C
  • minimum temperature: -5 °C
  • average temperature over a 24 hour period: 35 °C
  • average temperature over a 1 year period: 25 °C.

The capacitors, contactors and electrical connections dissipate heat:

  • Standard model (C160 ; C160MINI): 2.5 W/KVAr max.
  • Detuned model: (C50-L ; C100-L) : 6 W/KVAr max.

Air flow within the cubicle must be from the bottom to the top.

For natural ventilation, the cross-section of the top opening must be at least 1.1 times that of the bottom opening.

For forced ventilation, extractor type ventilators should be ftted on the top of the cubicle, therefore the size of the openings must be compatible with the protection index (IP).

For an ambient temperature of 40 °C, the temperature inside the cubicle can reach 55 °C.

The maximum continuous current (Imp) to be taken into account is:

  • 1.36 x In: equipment without detuning reactors
  • 1.5 x In: equipment with detuning reactors

Maintenance

Contactors

The verification of the wear of the main contacts, the integrity of the insertion resistors, their possible replacement (or the replacement of the entire contactor), must be performed at reasonable time intervals, taking into account that the number of maneuvers depends strongly from the fluctuations of the load that the equipment remade.

  • Contactors inspections every 10,000 operations

Warning

It is very important to carry out the checks described to avoid that, in case of excessive wear of the contacts, dangerous operating conditions can be determined, such as the destruction of contactors and capacitors.

An evaluation of the number of operations that the contactors can perform during the operation of the power factor correction equipment can be made at the time of installation by observing how many insertions are performed, in a given time period, by the contactors of the different capacitor banks to follow the load. If, for example, it is found that following a load fluctuations, the contactor of a battery performs 6 hourly entries, considering a working period of 8 hours a day, after about a year the contact wear must be checked:

in fact, 6 maneuvers / hour x 8hours x 220 days @ 10,000 maneuvers.
